\subsection*{Concordance: Math's 8-entry table vs Philosophy's 8-trap checklist}
\label{sec:S0-concordance}

The previous two subsections present two independently numbered eight-entry lists. These are \textbf{orthogonal axes of the epistemic-scope audit, not alternative numberings of the same list}. Philosophy's 8-trap checklist (\S\ref{sec:eightTraps}) enumerates hidden-assumption traps at the \emph{claim-writing} level, functioning as a reviewer-anticipation gate (manifestation $\rightarrow$ guard $\rightarrow$ residual risk). Math's 8-entry table (\Cref{tab:epistemic-traps}) enumerates \emph{mathematical characterisations} of concerns raised in an earlier Philosophy framing document, mapping each to the specific theorem, lemma, or assumption in Supp S1 that formalises it.

The two reinforce each other: Philosophy's checklist gives the reviewer the lint pass; Math's table gives the formal warrant for each guard. The concordance below eliminates the ``why are there two eight-entry lists?'' confusion.

\paragraph*{Usage.} The concordance is consulted only when a reviewer or future author cross-references one list and wants to know whether the other list speaks to the same concern. Routine manuscript reading follows either list independently: Philosophy's list for the writing-level lint, Math's table for the formal warrant.
